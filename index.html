How to Use TradeEdge

Follow this guide to get the most accurate and actionable analysis from every chart you upload.

Getting Started
1

Take a chart screenshot

Open your charting platform (TradingView, Binance, MT4, etc.) and take a screenshot of the chart you want analysed. The more of the chart that's visible, the better the analysis — ideally showing at least 3–6 months of price history with candles clearly visible.

💡 On Windows: Win+Shift+S · On Mac: Cmd+Shift+4 · On phone: screenshot button
2

Choose your analysis type

Pick one of the six analysis modes at the top of the page. Each one gives you a different type of breakdown — see the guide below for which to use and when.

3

Upload your chart — three ways to do it

Option A — Drag and drop: Drag the screenshot file from your computer straight onto the upload box.
Option B — Click to browse: Click the upload box and find the screenshot in your files.
Option C — Paste: Copy the screenshot to your clipboard and press Ctrl+V (or Cmd+V on Mac) — it pastes straight in. This is the fastest method.

💡 Ctrl+V paste works instantly after taking a Win+Shift+S screenshot
4

Add context in the text box (optional but highly recommended)

Tell the AI a few key details it can't see just from the image. For example: "BTC/USD, daily chart, I'm thinking about a long entry at current levels, volume has been rising for 3 days". The more context you give, the more specific and useful the analysis will be.

5

Click Analyse and read your breakdown

The analysis takes around 10–20 seconds. It will be saved automatically to your history at the bottom of the page so you can refer back to it at any time.

Which Analysis Type to Use
📈
Trend Analysis

Use this first on any new chart. Gives you the full picture — trend direction, strength, key support and resistance levels, and market structure.

🕰️
Multi-Timeframe

Use when you want to know the best timing to enter. Upload a chart and describe what you see on higher and lower timeframes in the context box.

🔍

Use when you think you can see a pattern forming but aren't sure. It will find and rate every pattern on the chart — flags, triangles, head and shoulders, wedges and more.

🎯

Use when you are close to placing a trade. Gives you a precise entry price, stop loss, and two take profit targets with exact risk/reward ratios.

Use when you have several assets and want to know which to focus on. Describe each one in the context box — it scores them 1 to 5 and tells you which to prioritise.

📝
Post-Trade Review

Use after closing a trade — win or loss. Upload the chart and describe what you did in the context box. It will honestly tell you what you did right and wrong and how to improve.

How to Get the Best Analysis
✓

Make sure the chart shows the asset name and timeframe

The AI needs to know what it's looking at. Most charting platforms show the asset name (e.g. BTC/USD) and timeframe (e.g. 1D, 4H) in the top left corner — make sure this is visible in your screenshot. If it isn't, mention it in the context box.

✓

Show enough price history

A chart showing only the last few hours gives very little context. Zoom out so at least 3–6 months of candles are visible. This allows the AI to identify major support and resistance levels, trends, and patterns that short-term charts miss entirely.

✓

Include volume bars if possible

Volume tells the AI whether a move is backed by conviction or is a low-quality fake-out. Most charting platforms show volume as bars at the bottom of the chart. Leave these visible in your screenshot for a stronger analysis.

💡 On TradingView: right-click chart → Add Indicator → Volume
✓

Use the context box for your trade idea

Don't just upload a blank chart. Tell the AI what you're thinking — "I'm considering a long here", "I think this is forming a double bottom", "I want to know if this rally has legs". This makes the analysis directly relevant to your decision rather than generic.

Do's and Don'ts
✓ DO
  • Start with Trend Analysis on every new asset
  • Use Trade Setup only when you are genuinely ready to place a trade
  • Read the full analysis before making any decision
  • Use Post-Trade Review after every loss to learn from it
  • Add context — the more detail the better
  • Check multiple timeframes for important trades
  • Treat the analysis as one input, not a signal to blindly follow
✗ DON'T
  • Upload a chart with only a few candles visible
  • Skip the context box on Trade Setup or Post-Trade Review
  • Treat AI analysis as guaranteed — markets are unpredictable
  • Use this on very low-liquidity or unknown assets
  • Enter a trade purely because the AI says to
  • Ignore the risk/reward ratios given in Trade Setup
  • Risk more than you can afford to lose on any single trade
Recommended Workflow for a Trade
1

Start with Trend Analysis on the daily chart

Get the big picture first. Is the asset in an uptrend, downtrend, or choppy range? This tells you which direction to be biased towards. Never fight the dominant trend on the daily.

2

Run Pattern Scanner to spot any forming setups

Upload the same chart and switch to Pattern Scanner. This will tell you if there is a recognisable pattern forming — flags, triangles, double bottoms etc — and what the expected move would be if it completes.

3

Drop to a lower timeframe and run Multi-Timeframe

Upload a 1H or 4H chart and ask for Multi-Timeframe analysis. This helps you find the right moment to enter — even if the daily trend is bullish, the 1H might be in a pullback, meaning you wait for the dip before buying.

4

Use Trade Setup to get your exact levels

When you are ready to place the trade, upload the chart one more time and run Trade Setup. This gives you a precise entry, stop loss, and two take profit targets. Write these levels down before you open the trade.

💡 Always set your stop loss in the platform before entering — don't rely on watching manually
5

After the trade closes, run Post-Trade Review

Win or lose, upload the chart after the trade closes and describe what happened. This is how you improve over time — understanding what you did well and what you could do better is more valuable than the profit or loss itself.
